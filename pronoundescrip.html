Arabic Personal Pronouns

Arabic Pronoun Description
أنا (ana) I, me
نحن (nahnu) we, us
أنتَ (anta) you (singular masculine), you (singular feminine)
أنتُما (antuma) you two (dual masculine/feminine)
أنتُم (antum) you (plural masculine), you (plural mixed gender)
أنتِ (anti) you (singular feminine)
أنتُما (antuma) you two (dual masculine/feminine)
أنتُنَّ (antunna) you (plural feminine)
هو (huwa) he, him
هما (humaa) they two (dual masculine/feminine)
هم (hum) they (masculine), they (mixed gender)
هي (hiya) she, her
هما (humaa) they two (dual masculine/feminine)
هن (hunna) they (feminine)

Arabic is a very unique language. So, the pronouns are also very unique. There are 14 pronouns but we can say 12 pronouns in total. Arabic dialects don't have this many pronouns. It is only in Modern Standard Arabic (MSA).
Arabic pronouns differ by number and gender. They have a singular form, a dual form, and a plural form. People might get confused because the dual form is unique. Dual forms of Arabic don't care about genders. They are gender-neutral. That's why there are only 12 pronouns, because the dual form of masculine and feminine is the same.
